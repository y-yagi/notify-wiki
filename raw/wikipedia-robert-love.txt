Robert M. Love is an American software engineer, author, and speaker born
around 1981 in south Florida. He's known for significant contributions to
the Linux kernel and open-source software.

Education: He attended Charles W. Flanagan High School, then the University
of Florida, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics and a Bachelor
of Science in Computer Science.

Kernel and Open Source Work: Love is credited with notable Linux kernel
contributions including the preemptive kernel, process scheduler, kernel
event layer, virtual memory subsystem, and inotify. He began kernel work
while still in college, employed at MontaVista Software.

Regarding inotify, the article notes he worked on it during his time at
Novell (after Ximian's acquisition), alongside other kernel and GNOME
projects like Beagle. It references an email he sent introducing inotify to
the kernel community, and an article he wrote titled "Kernel Korner - Intro
to inotify" for Linux Journal in 2005. The article doesn't mention dnotify
directly in connection with Love, though dnotify is referenced elsewhere as
a related Linux-only kernel interface.

Professional Background:
- Worked at MontaVista Software as a kernel hacker during college
- Joined Ximian in December 2003 as Senior Engineer in the Linux Desktop
  Group
- Led "Project Utopia" to improve hardware management integration on Linux
  desktops
- Became Chief Architect of SUSE Linux Enterprise Desktop after Novell
  acquired Ximian
- Left Novell in 2007 to join Google's then-unannounced Android team, where
  he built its novel shared memory subsystem, ashmem
- Served as Director of Engineering for Search Infrastructure at Google
  through May 2021
- Currently VP of Engineering at Toast, where he builds cloud platforms for
  restaurants

Writing: He authored Linux Kernel Development (three editions) and Linux
System Programming, and co-authored Linux in a Nutshell. He's also a
Contributing Editor for Linux Journal and has spoken at conferences
including linux.conf.au, FOSDEM, and GUADEC (as a keynote speaker).

He currently lives in Boston, Massachusetts, per the article (though marked
as needing citation).
